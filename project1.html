Pretending To Be Someone Else Online

This project demonstrates a DNS Hijacking and ARP Poisoning Attack and translates it into the business risk of stolen or misdirected data, showing that 88% of organizations face at least one DNS-based attack every year. It reveals how attackers can trick a device so it sends data to themselves instead of the real person or service, this data can then be read or changed. The write-up will help you understand these attacks and help security teams spot, respond to, and prevent them in real networks.
